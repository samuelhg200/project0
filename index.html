Swimming

What is so great about swimming?

Swimming is one of the most exhausting sports due to its naturally demanding nature,
is obvious when you consider that most of your time swimming is spent without the
ability to breath, which is considered quite essential in most other sports. Curiously
enough, breathing too much could make you lose time, spinning your head to take a sip
of air will most probably result in you increasing your body friction causing you to
lose some precious milisenconds. Abusing your ability to breath can turn out to be
horribly detrimental in a race! This is one of the things that make swimming such a
hard sport to domain. Finding the adequate frequency of strokes, finding the exact
frequency of breaths which allow you to stay strong without losing speed, finding
the best way to position your body and adapting this based on the length of the race
or the style you are going to swim. Mastering this underlying complexity is what allows
the best swimmers, well, to be the best swimmers.
